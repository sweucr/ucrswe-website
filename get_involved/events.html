Bourns Engineering Day
April 18th, 2020

Bourns Engineering Day is UCR's Bourns College of Engineering's largest outreach event, taking place on campus at the university and tailored for kids K-8th grade in Riverside and the surrounding areas. Our goal is to introduce kids to science, technology, engineering, art, and mathematics, and to better our home city by investing in the future. We want to inspire them to be curious and pursue answers through fun activities and speakers, all the while teaching them about the concepts and applications of important subjects.

If you have any questions, please email sweoutreach.ucr@gmail.com.
Evening With Industry
TBD, Winter 2020

Evening with Industry (EWI) is a large networking event meant to advance women in engineering by exposing them to industry professionals, resources and opportunities. It is an event that attracts talented students to come out and meet a large variety of companies while also allowing industries to introduce their company to potential employees. Be a part of EWI to network and have a chance to meet the best and the brightest students at UCR!

Engineering R'Future
October 19th, 2019
8:00 AM - 4:45 PM

Engineering R'Future (ERF) is an event dedicated towards high school women to give them a better perspective on the different engineering majors that UCR offers. Throughout the day, students will be guided by passionate UCR engineering students and have the opportunity to meet with other high school women interested in engineering. Students will gain a comprehensive understanding of engineering and college to help make their career decisions clearer.

If you have any questions, please email sweoutreach.ucr@gmail.com.
Research Networking Night
November 21th, 2019

Research Networking Night (RNN) is a joint event held by the Society of Women Engineers (SWE), American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE), and Window to the Brain (WttB). The purpose of this event is to bring faculty, professors, graduate students and undergraduate students from sciences and engineering together. Our goal is for motivated undergraduates to make connections, conceptualize the expectations of the research field and to discover possible research positions for the future.

If you have any questions, please email ucr.rnn@gmail.com.
Rose Hack
TBD, 2020

Rose Hack is UCR's first women-centric hackathon, hosten by Society of Women Engineers (SWE) the Association for Computing Machinery - Women's Chapter (ACM-W). An ode to Rosemary Bourns, co-founder of the Bourns College of Engineering, Rose Hack's mission is to empower women and underrepresented groups in engineering.

If you have any questions, please email rosehackucr@gmail.com.
