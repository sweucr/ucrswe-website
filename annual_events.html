ANNUAL EVENTS

Every year, SWE hosts annual events that occur throughout Fall, Winter, and Spring Quarter! These events include, EWI, RNN, ERF, and BNGO which cover professional, academic, and K-12 development!





Evening with Industry (EWI)

Evening with Industry (EWI) is the largest student-run networking event at the University of California, Riverside (UCR). It is designed to advance engineering students’ careers by networking with company recruiters and engineers looking to hire recent grads or interns.

Event Info: Check back in Fall 2026!

Research Networking Night (RNN)

Research Networking Night (RNN) is an event held by the Society of Women Engineers (SWE). The purpose of this event is to bring faculty, professors, graduate students and undergraduate students from sciences and engineering together. Our goal is for motivated undergraduates to make connections, conceptualize the expectations of the research field and to discover possible research positions for the future.

Event Info: Check back in Winter 2027!

Engineering R'Future (ERF)

Engineering R'Future (ERF) is an event dedicated towards high school women to give them a better perspective on the different engineering majors that UCR offers. Throughout the day, students will be guided by passionate UCR engineering students and have the opportunity to meet with other high school women interested in engineering. Students will gain a comprehensive understanding of engineering and college to help make their career decisions clearer.

Event Info: Check back in Winter 2027!

Bourns Engineering Day (BNGO)

Bourns Engineering Day is UCR's Bourns College of Engineering's largest outreach event, taking place on campus at the university and tailored for kids K-8th grade in Riverside and the surrounding areas. Our goal is to introduce kids to science, technology, engineering, art, and mathematics, and to better our home city by investing in the future. We want to inspire them to be curious and pursue answers through fun activities and speakers, all the while teaching them about the concepts and applications of important subjects.

Event Info: Check back in Spring 2026!
